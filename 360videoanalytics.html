360 Video Analytics for Nonprofit Fundraising

Problem

How might immersive 360 video create better marketing or fundraising materials for nonprofits or social enterprises?
How might we collect and visualize user data in mobile VR headsets?
How might we collect data across multiple mobile VR headsets?

At the Accenture Tech Labs, I participated in a side project to better enable social impact organizations to adopt 360 videos and mobile VR experiences. In addition to creating better mobile VR content, we also wanted to collect user data and visualize the focus of the user in the 360 frame of the video.

My role for this project was designing and developing the entire Unity custom application, testing the application and data collection with users, integrating the project with the backend AWS architecture, and contributing to the front-end data visualization.

Process

  • Research 360 videos in mobile VR (hardware, software, and current trending videos from New York Times and Ryot)
  • Sketch on iPad
  • Prototype in VR on the samsung Gear, and the 360 fly
  • Prototype user data visualization in three.js front-end
  • Test with users to understand how the view data visualizations in immersive 3D
  • Create MVP using Unity and C#
  • Integrate with AWS to collect data across multiple devices simultaneously
  • Test with users, get feedback!
  • Iterate & repeat!

Tools

  • Rapid iterations & Feedback cycles in the Samsung Gear VR
  • Sketching on iPad Pro
  • Three.js
  • Sketching
  • Unity – Game Engine & C# Scripting
  • AWS - Dynamo & S3

Outcome

  • Designed and developed the custom Unity application for 360 video selector, and data collection
  • Integrated AWS S3 with Unity application to “send data to the cloud”
  • Custom three.js data visualization front end to show what users were focused on in the headset
  • First hand experience with “for cause” storytelling in 360 video
  • Cut 360 video user testing cycle by half as a result of direct UX data collection and data insights!
  • Became a SME (subject matter expert) in 360 video and mobile vr data analytics

The work was a successful exploration and prototype in 360 video storytelling and collecting user data in mobile VR. During user testing, we were able to conclude that users felt closer to the content being shown in VR. However the lack of control in user view in the headset made the user data collection and the visualization the most important outcome of the project. Our partners and clients were very interested in better iterating and creating the 360 stories and content with the help of analytics and data insights.

The work was also applied to corporate 360 video marketing examples in the Labs. It was shared with multiple c-suite clients and featured in the Labs tour, as well as showcased in a large automotive client’s IT roadshow. It also led to a small pilot and prototype with Accenture HR, to film “a day in the life” 360 videos from current employees across the company to share with new hires during on boarding!

Looking Forward

360 video and content creation is a challenge! Because of the amount of the user freedom in the headset, it is critical to get clear insights from the user experience to better craft the story being told. However, after this project, I’m certain that 360 video and mobile VR are a more effective and persuasive medium for nonprofits or social impact organizations to gain followers and better tell their story. In an ideal world, I would love to better apply data and analytics to 360 video marketing materials and create an open source version of the project!
