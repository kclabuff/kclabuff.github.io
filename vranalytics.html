VR Analytics & VR Data Visualization

Problem

How might we track interactions and engagement in a virtual environment?
How might we record and visualize this data in VR or on a 2D screen?
How could we apply storytelling methodologies in VR to show data?

In the Accenture Tech Labs, I was tasked with the challenge to develop a foundation for analytics and data visualization in virtual reality, ‘VR’. The purpose was to be able to track and analyze a user’s behavior while in a VR headset. What does a user focus on? Where do they get “stuck”?

With these data insights, we could improve user experience and demos while in a headset. For clients, we could also apply VR data analytics to improve VR training scenarios!

For this project, I worked in the CPG (Consumer Packaged Goods) industry to better understand how users would move and interact with a virtual store.

Process

Ideate & brainstorm with team with a whiteboard
Sketch on iPad
Prototype in VR with the HTC Vive and Google Tiltbrush
Test with users to understand how the view data visualizations in immersive 3D
Create MVP using Unity and C#
Test with users, get feedback!
Iterate & repeat!

Tools

  • Unity 3D
  • C# Scripting
  • HTC Vive
  • Sketching
  • Prototype in VR - Google Tilt Brush

Outcome

Show user movement through heatmap trails
Show gaze interactions using product heatmap, and “gaze rays”
Test “audio insights” integration to relay information

I was able to create a total of 5 unique data visualization MVPs, and a C-suite client facing demo that I presented in the labs on a weekly basis! Functionality included showing user movement through “trails” in mid-air or on the ground, the ability to show “gaze” or where the user is looking in the store and highlight products based on gaze time length on that object. I also experimented with adding hidden audio “insights” or hotspots into the scene to relay important information to the user, or direct them around the scene in a more “guided” experience.

Looking Forward

Overall, I learned a lot from the VR analytics project, and it successfully delivered a working prototype for the Lab and Accenture clients. However, a lot more creativity could have been injected into the process from the beginning. For example, during our brainstorming session, we centered around inspiration from 2D data visualization (d3, graphs, tables, etc.) If forced to think outside of the box (3D pun intended), we could have created even more of a range of 3D data visualization prototypes. I got a hint of this creativity given the unique medium through the audio VR guide experiment, but even more exploration would have been great!
